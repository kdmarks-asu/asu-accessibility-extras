Keyboard-Only Access

"The power of the Web is in its universality. Access by everyone regardless of disability is an essential aspect."

--Tim Berners-Lee

Imagine that one day you break your computer mouse, or hurt your hand and can't hold a mouse...or catch pink eye from the neighbor kids and your screen is blurry. In all these scenarios, you might be compelled to rely on your computer's keyboard to navigate the Internet.

 

For people with low or no vision or who have mobility limitations, using a mouse is often impossible. They must rely exclusively on the keyboard (or assistive technology with keyboard-like functionality). This is why it's so important for web developers to test their websites and applications to make sure they function as well without a mouse as they do with one.

Keyboard-Only Exercise

Put aside your mouse (or refrain from using your touchpad). Use only:

  • Tab key: to move forward (use Shift + Tab to reverse)
  • Arrow keys: to move around in widgets like menus
  • Enter/Return key or Space bar: to select
  • Escape key: to close popup windows or when desperate

You may use your mouse to click on the links, but when the page opens, use only your keyboard to navigate:

  1. https://weather.com/
  2. https://www.prevention.com/
  3. https://www.wsj.com/

 

How did you do?

  • Could you tell where you were on the page at all times? (A focus indicator, usually a dotted or blue border, should be present as you tab.)
  • Could you close popup windows?
  • Could you get to menus and select menu items?

If you thought the exercise was frustrating, you aren't alone. For people with motor or vision disabilities, navigating the Internet with only their keyboard is frequently a precarious venture.
